# E9 DGI One-Sample Constant C0 Audit + Photon-Convention Flip-Boundary Spread (r1)

Setup: C0 = E||Z||^2/S2 - 1 with Z(x)=[I(x)-mu]*B/sigma^2, B=sum_x' I(x')O(x'), measured from 20000 single-frame pattern draws per ensemble (K=1024 pixels), for 4 pattern ensembles (uniform[0,1], binary 0/1, signed Rademacher, Gaussian(mu=0.5,sigma=0.2) hard-clipped to [0,1]) x 5 objects spanning K_eff (letter_L, digit_2, ring, stripe, natural_patch; K_eff=59.8-864.3). ||Z||^2=(B/sigma^2)^2*||I-mu||^2 exactly (Z is a per-draw scalar times a fixed centered-pattern vector), so the estimator needs no explicit (n_draws,K) Z matrix. mu/sigma/beta_4/gamma_3 are the SAME large-sample empirical moments used to seed the formula, not independently-sourced textbook values.
Formula-vs-measured: C0 = K+beta_4-2+K_eff*[K*(mu/sigma)^2+2*gamma_3*(mu/sigma)] agrees with the direct E||Z||^2/S2-1 estimate to mean |rel err|=0.35%, worst 2.01% (ensemble=rademacher, object=natural_patch, C0_measured=1002.7, C0_formula=1023.2) across all 20 (ensemble, object) cells -- confirming the derivation (only signed Rademacher has mu=0 among these 4 ensembles, so the K_eff-dependent cross term is non-trivial for uniform/binary/gaussian_clipped, and C0 itself ranges from O(K) for Rademacher to O(K_eff * K) ~ 1e5-1e6 for uniform/binary/gaussian with the higher-K_eff objects -- consistent in order of magnitude with the leverage*N ~ 7.4-7.7e5 range independently measured for the uniform-pattern random_dgi arm in the Fig. 4 bridge).
Photon-convention weighting (documented, simplified add-on -- NOT part of the leading-order rho* formula itself, which has no explicit photon term; C0 is a pure pattern-statistics constant with no photon dependence, per Part A, so the convention instead inflates the EFFECTIVE drift variance that survives blind correction): Phi_frame(N) is defined per convention as equal_exposure=Phi_ref (fixed per-frame flux, total budget grows with N), equal_count=Phi_ref*N0/N (fixed TOTAL budget Phi_ref*N0 redistributed across N frames), fixed_flux=Phi_ref*sqrt(N0/N) (geometric-mean intermediate), with Phi_ref=8, N0=1024=K, evaluated at N=4096=4*N0 (chosen != N0 so the three conventions differ numerically -- at N=N0 all three collapse to the same Phi_frame by construction). s_eff^2=s^2+kappa_s/Phi_frame with s=0.1, kappa_s=0.05, and rho*=2*C0_measured/(N*K_eff*s_eff^2). Spread of rho* across the 3 conventions (max/min ratio) ranges 2.154-2.154 across cells (mean 2.154) -- non-trivial (kappa_s/Phi_frame is comparable to s^2 at this Phi_ref by construction) and IDENTICAL across ensembles/objects for fixed (N, s, kappa_s, Phi_ref) since the convention only rescales the shared denominator s_eff^2, not C0 or K_eff; a photon-starved acquisition (equal_count, low Phi_ref) raises rho* relative to a well-exposed one (equal_exposure), i.e. it shrinks the slow-drift regime where pairwise Hadamard remains the safe choice.
