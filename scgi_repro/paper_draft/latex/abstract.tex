\begin{abstract}
For a fixed square linear inverse with known gain and matched budget, an ordered orthogonal scan minimizes conditioning-driven amplification of additive noise; with unknown slow multiplicative gain and unconstrained objects, the same deterministic chronology can create an exact gain--object ambiguity. We resolve this paradox with an identifiability theory of blind gain--object separation, distinguishing algebraic identifiability, statistical identifiability, and estimation conditioning. Every square invertible design fails algebraically (modulo global scale); tall designs restore uniqueness generically at $N \ge K+p$ frames for a $p$-dimensional drift class. Independently, a stationarity anchor (Theorem~B) pins the relative gain statistically: once the object carrier is stationary in time, slow modulation of the record can only be gain. Randomized Hadamard/SRHT acquisition preserves orthogonal conditioning while supplying, under Walsh-flatness or a successful random permutation of a sufficiently spread object, the stationary or exchangeable carrier the proposed windowed estimator needs; an exact finite-noise relative-MSE identity then yields a design chart with a computable flip boundary between ordered and randomized acquisition, whose gain-known skeleton is validated on the simulation grid with no free parameters. In frame-matched simulations, raw ordered Hadamard leaves a non-decaying blind gain error of 0.94--0.99 across all averaging windows, where randomized arms reach 0.007--0.015, and SRHT recovers $+5.45$ dB under slow drift; under the log-domain estimator of Theorem~B the raw ordered arm still cannot fall below 0.71 (descriptive medians; clustered 95\% CIs in Sec.~9). The acquisition rule: randomize the carrier's chronology --- make the object stop pretending to be time.
\end{abstract}
