\begin{abstract}
With known gain, an ordered orthogonal scan controls additive-noise amplification; with unknown slow multiplicative gain and unconstrained objects, the same deterministic chronology creates an exact gain--object ambiguity. We distinguish algebraic identifiability, statistical relative-gain recovery, and reconstruction conditioning. Every square invertible design fails algebraically modulo scale; tall designs are generically exact at $N\ge K+p$ for a $p$-dimensional drift class, with a proved generic ordinary-local threshold at $N=K+p-1$ for $K\ge2$. For a stationary carrier triangular array, a windowed log estimator is consistent when its actual smoothing bias and window variance vanish; in a fixed iid-Gaussian quotient experiment its interior rate is minimax, while photon-limited recovery is treated separately by an oracle-known-carrier calibrated Poisson theorem. Full randomized Hadamard acquisition preserves orthogonal conditioning: pixel signs and pixel permutation control ensemble covariance through a Walsh-flatness criterion, whereas random acquisition order supplies finite-population exchangeability. An exact raw-MSE reconstruction identity then yields a flip-boundary diagnostic. On identical OU traces, the theorem-faithful true-$S_1$ boundary agrees with all 40 observed crossings within median/max factors $1.020/1.037$; the production median-normalized arm gives $1.044/1.200$ and is a robustness check rather than the same-estimator proof. In frame-matched simulations, raw ordered Hadamard leaves blind gain error $0.94$--$0.99$ across averaging windows, randomized arms reach $0.007$--$0.015$, and randomized Hadamard gains $5.45$ dB under slow drift. The acquisition rule is to randomize the carrier's chronology --- make the object stop pretending to be time.
\end{abstract}
